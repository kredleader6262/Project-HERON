Glossary

Trading & Regulatory

Wash sale §1091
IRS rule: if you sell a security at a loss and buy it (or a "substantially identical" one) within 30 days before or after, the loss is disallowed and the basis of the replacement shares is adjusted. HERON pre-checks every order against recent losing closes and blocks trades that would trigger one.
Pattern Day Trader PDT
FINRA rule: four or more day trades in a rolling 5-business-day window flags the account as a PDT, requiring $25k equity minimum. HERON tracks day-trade count and warns/blocks near the limit.
Day trade
Opening and closing the same position on the same trading day. Counts toward PDT.
Settlement T+1
Equity trades settle one business day after execution (as of May 2024). Cash is not available for another buy until settlement.
Good faith violation GFV
Selling a security bought with unsettled funds before those funds have settled. Three GFVs in 12 months = 90-day restriction.
Market session
Four phases shown in the status bar: pre (04:00–09:30 ET), open (09:30–16:00 ET, regular hours), after (16:00–20:00 ET), closed (overnight + weekends).

Strategies & Signals

PEAD Post-Earnings Announcement Drift
Documented anomaly: stocks that beat (miss) earnings tend to drift in that direction for weeks after the announcement, beyond the initial price jump. HERON's pead_v1 strategy scans for earnings surprises + positive drift confirmation and holds for a minimum number of days.
Momentum breakout
Strategy family that enters on price breaking above a defined resistance level (e.g., N-day high) with volume confirmation. HERON's momentum_breakout_v1 is the canonical implementation.
Baseline strategy
A locked-in control variant (named *_baseline) kept deliberately simple and unchanged. Used as a reference to detect whether the "improved" version is actually outperforming — if not, the baseline wins.
Candidate
A ticker surfaced by the research layer that might be worth trading under some strategy. Has a disposition: pending, accepted, or rejected. Accepting a candidate does not automatically create an order — it graduates it to the strategy's queue.
Thesis
Short natural-language rationale attached to each candidate. "Why do we think this is a trade?" Written by the LLM during research; stored in the journal for post-mortems.
Watchlist
The universe of tickers the system is allowed to look at. Defined in config. Candidates outside the watchlist are filtered out early.

Execution & Orders

Idempotent order
An order that can be safely resubmitted without creating duplicates. HERON achieves this with a deterministic client_order_id = {strategy}_{utc_ms}_{ticker}_{side}. Before submitting, the executor queries whether that ID already exists.
client_order_id
A broker-accepted string the client generates to tag an order. Prevents duplicate fills if the network hiccups mid-submission. Never let the broker assign it silently.
Fill
The actual price and quantity at which an order executed. May differ from the order price (slippage). The fill_price is what HERON records in the journal as the trade entry.
Virtual stop
A stop-loss that HERON monitors locally and fires a market order when triggered, rather than resting with the broker. Reduces exposure to broker stop-hunting and enables custom logic (e.g., ATR-based trailing). Polled — not event-driven.
Stale quote
A last-trade quote older than 10 seconds. HERON's kill switch refuses to submit orders on stale quotes — the market may have moved significantly and the sizing/stop math would be wrong.
Paper vs. live
paper = Alpaca's simulated execution (no real money). live = real account. Strategy state must be explicitly promoted to LIVE before paper mode is dropped.
Reconciliation
Periodic sync between HERON's journal and the broker's actual positions/orders. If they disagree, something went wrong — the journal is updated and an event logged.

Risk & Safety

Kill switch
Any condition that halts new order submission: stale quotes, cost ceiling breach, broker connectivity loss, or manual operator halt.
Operator-gated
No code path autonomously increases risk exposure. New strategies, size increases, and state promotions require explicit operator approval via the dashboard or CLI.
Position sizing
How many shares to buy, derived from: per-strategy max_capital_pct, account equity, entry price, stop distance, and a max-loss-per-trade cap.
Drawdown budget
Per-strategy limit on cumulative unrealized + realized loss before the strategy is auto-paused. Expressed as a percentage of allocated capital.
Max capital pct
The fraction of total account equity a strategy is allowed to commit at peak. Prevents any single strategy from dominating the book.
Min hold days
Minimum days a position must stay open before the strategy may close it on signal. Prevents whipsaw and short-term-gains tax treatment on some strategies.

Research & LLM

LLM boundary
The LLM researches and recommends. The LLM never decides entries, exits, or position sizes. Deterministic code owns execution. Violating this boundary is a bug.
Local LLM (Ollama)
Qwen 2.5 7B running on localhost. Used for cheap, high-volume passes: news triage, sentiment classification, relevance filtering.
Cloud LLM (Claude)
Anthropic's Claude (Sonnet + Haiku). Used for higher-stakes passes: candidate thesis generation, strategy proposals, audits. Costs money — budgeted.
Token budget
Monthly cap on LLM spend (default $45). Tracked in real time; dashboard status bar shows current month-to-date %. Hard halt at 100% — no new cloud LLM calls.
Prompt injection
Adversarial text embedded in scraped content (news articles, filings) designed to manipulate LLM output. All scraped text is treated as untrusted input and sanitized before reaching the LLM.
Trust score
Composite metric (0–100) of the research pipeline's reliability: source diversity, LLM agreement, audit pass rate, and staleness. Shown on the Audits page as a ring gauge.
Memorization contamination
When a backtest window overlaps with the LLM's training cutoff, the model may "remember" outcomes, biasing the research. Flagged on backtest reports.

Architecture

Five-layer architecture
Data → Research → Strategy → Execution → Journal/Dashboard. Each layer has strict interfaces; any layer can be swapped without touching the others.
Journal
SQLite database (WAL mode) that records every decision, order, fill, event, cost, and audit. Single source of truth. "The journal is the product."
Strategy state
Lifecycle of a strategy: PROPOSED → awaiting operator review; PAPER → running in simulation; LIVE → real money; PAUSED → halted temporarily; RETIRED → permanently off.
Tailscale boundary
The dashboard is never publicly exposed. Access is gated by Tailscale VPN — only devices on your tailnet can reach it.
Scheduler APScheduler
Cron-like job runner for periodic tasks: data refresh, research passes, reconciliation, end-of-day reports.
Hot path
The code path from signal → sized order → broker. Must be fast, deterministic, and LLM-free.

Metrics & Backtesting

Sharpe ratio
Risk-adjusted return: (strategy return − risk-free rate) / return volatility. Higher is better; >1.0 is decent, >2.0 is excellent. Naive Sharpes mislead on low trade counts.
Max drawdown
Largest peak-to-trough decline of equity curve during the test period. Key risk metric — tells you how bad it got.
Win rate
Fraction of trades closed profitable. Alone is uninformative — a 40% win rate with 3:1 reward/risk beats 70% at 1:2.
P&L
Profit and loss. net_pnl in HERON is after fees/slippage. pnl_pct is return on capital committed to that trade.
Walk-forward test
Backtesting method that re-tunes parameters on a rolling training window and validates on the next out-of-sample window. Defends against overfitting compared to a single in-sample optimization.
Look-ahead bias
Backtest flaw where the strategy accidentally uses information that wasn't available at decision time (e.g., same-day close to decide same-day open). Silently inflates results.

Ops & Status

System state
The dot in the top-left: OK (green) all clear; WARN (orange) non-blocking issue — cost warning, wash-sale exposure, or pending items; HALT (red) order submission disabled.
Inbox
Strategies in PROPOSED state awaiting operator approval. The badge next to the nav link shows the count.
Disposition
A candidate's current fate: pending (awaiting review), accepted (promoted), rejected (killed with reason).
Audit
Post-hoc verification of a research or execution decision. Catches drift in LLM behavior, unexpected costs, or anomalous trades. Trust score aggregates audit results.
Resilience
Dashboard view showing secret hygiene (are API keys leaked in logs?), startup audits (did the last launch pass checks?), and graceful shutdowns (was state flushed cleanly?).
Event
A journaled occurrence with a severity (info/warn/error). Every meaningful decision, failure, or state change produces one. "If the journal doesn't know about it, it's a bug."
